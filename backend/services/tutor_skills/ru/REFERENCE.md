# Curriculum reference — Russian (ru)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Gender of nouns (он, она, оно) — Refer back to people and things
- Это — this is / it is — Introduce and identify things
- Present tense (читать, говорить) — Talk about what people do
- Prepositional case (location: в / на) — Say where something is
- Accusative case (direct object) — Say what the action is done to
- Possessive pronouns (мой, моя, моё) — Say whose something is
- Past tense (-л) — Talk about what happened
- Genitive of absence (у меня нет …) — Say what you have and don't have

## A2
- Dative case (indirect object) — Say to whom or for whom something is done
- Мне нравится — liking with the dative — Say what you like and don't like
- Instrumental case (with, by means of) — Say with whom, with what, or as what
- Aspect pairs: imperfective and perfective — Distinguish a process from a completed result
- Future with буду (imperfective future) — Talk about ongoing or repeated future actions
- Perfective future (прочитаю, напишу) — Promise or predict a completed result
- Verbs of motion: идти vs ходить — Say where someone is going on foot
- Verbs of motion: ехать vs ездить — Say where someone is going by vehicle
- Imperative (читай! говорите!) — Tell someone to do something
- Reflexive verbs (-ся) — Talk about study, daily routines, and events
- Animate accusative (вижу брата) — Talk about people as objects of an action
- Time expressions (в понедельник, утром) — Say when something happens

## B1
- Comparatives (интереснее, лучше) — Compare people and things
- Superlatives (самый) — Single out the best, biggest, or most important
- Short-form adjectives (рад, готов, должен) — Describe temporary states and obligations
- Свой — one's own — Refer back to the subject's own things
- Numerals + case (два часа, пять лет) — Count things correctly
- Ordinals and dates (первое января) — Name dates and say when something happened
- Conditional бы — Talk about hypothetical and polite wishes
- Relative clauses: который — Describe things with a full clause
- Purpose clauses: чтобы + infinitive — Say why you do something
- Reported speech (что, ли) — Report what someone said or asked
- Impersonal constructions (надо, можно, нельзя) — Express necessity, permission, and prohibition
- Prefixed verbs of motion (прийти, уйти, выйти) — Describe arrivals, departures, and passing through

## B2
- Aspect in the imperative and prohibitions — Command, invite, and forbid with the right nuance
- Себя and сам — Point the action back at the doer, or stress who did it
- Present active participles (-ущий/-ющий) — Describe someone by what they are doing
- Past active participles (-вший) — Describe someone by what they did
- Past passive participles (-нный/-тый) — Describe things by what was done to them
- Short passive participles (дом построен) — Report results and states — the Russian passive
- Imperfective gerund (читая) — Describe two actions happening at once
- Perfective gerund (прочитав) — Say what happened before the main action
- Verbal prefixes (пере-, до-, за-) — Build precise new verbs from ones you know
- Negative pronouns (никто, ничего) — Say that nobody, nothing, or never applies

## C1
- Participial style: replacing который — Write compact formal prose
- General-factual imperfective (Вы читали…?) — Ask and state whether something ever happened
- Чтобы + past: wishes and demands — Say what you want someone else to do
- Reflexive passive (дом строится) — Describe processes without naming the doer
- Declension of complex numerals — Handle numbers inside case constructions

## C2
- Formal participial and nominal style — Read and produce official, academic Russian
- Aspect nuance: annulled results (открывал vs открыл) — Signal whether a result still holds
- Discourse particles (же, ведь, -то) — Colour a sentence with attitude and shared knowledge
- Emphatic focus: именно and это-clefts — Put the spotlight on one element of the sentence
