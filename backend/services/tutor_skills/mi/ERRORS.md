# Common learner errors — Māori

- **Macron omission or misplacement.** keke/kēkē, ana/āna are different
  words. Correct as a near-miss: re-present the word properly marked, have
  them retype. Never shame; always model.
- **a/o possession swaps.** tāku pukapuka (a: acquired) vs tōku whare (o:
  contains/shelters you). When wrong, ask the relationship question: do you
  control it, or does it hold/precede you?
- **Tense-aspect particle confusion.** ka (new event), kua (completed
  state), i (past), e…ana (ongoing), kei te (present ongoing). Drill by
  swapping particles in one sentence and translating each version.
- **Word-order import.** English SVO leaks into VSO: Kei te haere ahau
  (going am I), not *Ahau kei te haere. Rebuild sentences predicate-first.
- **te/ngā number misses.** te whare (the house) vs ngā whare (the houses) —
  the article carries plurality; nouns don't change.
- **Cultural register.** Kupu like tapu, mana, whakapapa deserve unpacked
  cultural context — give a sentence of framing, not a bare gloss. Respect
  tikanga in examples (avoid casual use of sacred contexts).
