# Curriculum reference — Māori (mi)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Pronouns (au, koe, ia…) — Say who you are talking about
- Te and ngā — the articles — Point to one thing or many
- He — saying what something is — Classify people and things
- Ko — naming and identifying — Name a specific person, place, or thing
- Kei te — happening now — Say what is happening right now
- I — the past — Say what happened
- Ka — what happens next — Talk about what will happen
- Kei — where things are — Say where someone or something is
- Negation with kāore — Say what is not so
- Questions (he aha, ko wai, kei hea) — Ask about things, people, and places
- Possession: the a and o categories — Say whose something is — the Māori way
- Statives (he nui, he pai) — Describe people and things

## A2
- Kua — the perfect — Say something has (already) happened
- E … ana — the progressive — Say an action is/was in progress
- Dual & plural pronouns (māua, tātou…) — Talk about two people, and 'we' including or excluding the listener
- Possessives and the a/o categories — Say 'my / your / his' with the right vowel
- Passive verbs (kua kainga) — Focus on what is done, not who does it
- Prepositions (ki, i, hei) — Say to, from, and 'as / for the purpose of'
- Nominalisation (te haerenga) — Turn an action into a noun ('the going', 'the singing')
- Nō and Mō — 'of/from' and 'for' — Say who something belongs to and who it is for
- Counting things and people — Say how many, of things and of people
- Me — 'should' and gentle instruction — Suggest or mildly direct an action

## B1
- Kia — wishes, purpose, and 'be…!' — Express a goal, a wish, or an encouragement
- Negating tenses (kāore i te, kīhai i) — Say something is not / was not happening
- Actor emphatic — Nā … i (past) and Mā … e (future) — Highlight who did or will do an action
- The causative whaka- — Turn a state or action into 'make/cause to…'
- Relative clauses (te tangata e mōhio ana au) — Describe a noun with a whole clause
- Conditions & purpose (ki te, mehemea) — Say 'if' and 'in order to'
- Directional particles (mai, atu, ake, iho) — Show which way an action moves

## B2
- Taea — 'can' (ka taea e au) — Say what can and can't be done
- Kāore anō … kia — not yet — Say something hasn't happened yet
- I te — the past progressive — Describe what was happening
- Hei — purpose and 'as a' — Say what something is for
- Imperatives and kaua — do and don't — Command, invite, and prohibit
- Ai — habitual and consequent clauses — Link habits, reasons, and 'that's when/where/why'

## C1
- Nā and Mā — ownership and agency predicates — Say whose something is and who will do what
- Ahakoa, nō te mea, engari — weaving clauses — Concede, explain, and contrast
- Ko-fronting — focus and identity — Put the important thing first

## C2
- Mihi — the shape of formal greeting — Open an encounter the Māori way
- Whakataukī — proverb patterns — Recognize and deploy the classic sayings
