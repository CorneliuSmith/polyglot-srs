# Common learner errors — French

- **Auxiliary choice.** Motion/change verbs and all reflexives take être
  (elle est arrivée, agreement included). When wrong, check: is it the
  être-set or reflexive?
- **Participle agreement with avoir.** Agreement only with a PRECEDING direct
  object: les lettres que j'ai écritES. Advanced but card-relevant; coach
  when it appears.
- **Silent morphology.** ils parlent = il parle to the ear. Train
  listening/writing pairs; spelling carries grammar French speech hides.
- **Negative article reduction.** je n'ai pas DE pain (not du). Drill
  positive→negative transformations.
- **Subjunctive triggers.** il faut que tu VIENNES; teach frames as chunks.
- **y/en omission.** "Tu vas à Paris? — Oui, j'y vais." Force answers that
  need y/en.
- **Gender guessing.** Endings help (-tion f, -age m) but teach each noun
  with its article; correct silently and move on — gender is long-haul.
- **Spoken vs written register.** Dropped ne, on for nous: name both
  registers so input (spoken) and cards (written) don't seem to conflict.
