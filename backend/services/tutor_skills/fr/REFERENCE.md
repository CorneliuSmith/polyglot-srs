# Curriculum reference — French (fr)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Subject pronouns (je, tu, il…) — Say who you are talking about
- Gender and number of nouns — Name people and things, one or many
- Definite articles (le / la / les) — Point to a specific thing
- Indefinite articles (un / une / des) — Mention something for the first time
- Être — present (je suis, tu es…) — Say who or what someone is
- Avoir — present (j'ai faim) — Say what you have — and how you feel
- Present tense of -er verbs — Talk about what people do
- -ir and -re verbs (finir, vendre) — Finish, choose, wait, sell — the other two families
- Negation with ne … pas — Say what is not so
- Il y a — there is / there are — Say what exists or is available
- Question words (où, comment, quand…) — Ask about places, people, time, and reasons
- Adjective agreement — Describe people and things correctly

## A2
- Passé composé with avoir — Talk about completed past actions
- Passé composé with être — Use être for movement and reflexive verbs in the past
- The imperfect (l'imparfait) — Describe ongoing or habitual past actions
- The future (le futur simple) — Talk about what will happen
- Direct object pronouns (le, la, les…) — Replace the object with a short pronoun
- Indirect pronouns, y and en (lui, leur, y, en) — Replace 'to someone', a place, or a quantity
- Comparative & superlative (plus… que, le plus) — Compare things
- Reflexive verbs (se laver) — Say the subject acts on itself
- Partitive articles (du, de la, des) — Say 'some' of an uncountable thing
- Possessive & demonstrative adjectives (mon, ce…) — Say 'my / this' and agree with the noun

## B1
- The subjunctive (le subjonctif) — Express necessity, wish, emotion, and doubt
- The conditional (le conditionnel) — Say what you would do; be polite
- Two object pronouns (je le lui donne) — Combine 'it' and 'to him' before the verb
- Relative pronouns (qui, que, où, dont) — Join two clauses into one
- Near future & recent past (aller / venir de) — Say what's about to happen or just happened
- The pluperfect (le plus-que-parfait) — Set one past action before another
- Reported speech (le discours indirect) — Report what someone said or asked

## B2
- The passive voice (la voix passive) — Say an action is done, and by whom
- Hypotheticals with si (si + imparfait) — Say what would happen if…
- The gerund (le gérondif: en faisant) — Say 'while / by doing' something
- Subjunctive after conjunctions — Use the subjunctive after 'although, so that, before…'
- Irregular comparatives (meilleur, mieux, pire) — Compare with 'better' and 'worse'
- Logical connectors (pourtant, donc, cependant) — Link ideas: contrast, cause, and consequence

## C1
- The past conditional (le conditionnel passé) — Say what would have happened
- The past subjunctive & sequence of tenses — Use the subjunctive for a completed action
- The literary past (le passé simple) — Recognize the tense of novels and history

## C2
- Emphasis (c'est… qui / que, ce qui… c'est) — Foreground a word with a cleft construction
- Elevated register (subjonctif littéraire, ne explétif) — Recognize and use the formal written layer
