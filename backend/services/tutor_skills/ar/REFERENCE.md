# Curriculum reference — Arabic (ar)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Personal pronouns (أنا، أنتَ، هو…) — Say who you are talking about
- Nominal sentences — no 'to be' — Describe and identify without a verb
- The definite article ال — sun and moon letters — Point to a specific thing
- Gender and ة (tāʾ marbūṭa) — Name people by who they are
- Demonstratives (هذا / هذه) — Introduce and point at things
- Adjective agreement — Describe things correctly
- Roots and patterns (ك ت ب) — See how Arabic words are built
- Iḍāfa — possession by pairing — Say whose or of what something is
- Attached pronouns (كتابي، كتابك…) — Say my, your, his — in one word
- Present tense (أدرس، يعمل…) — Talk about what people do
- Negation (لا / ليس) — Say what is not so
- Questions (هل، ما، من، أين) — Ask about things, people, places, and time

## A2
- Past tense (الماضي) — Talk about completed actions
- The future (سـ / سوف) — Talk about what will happen
- Sound plurals (ون/ين، ات) — Make regular plurals of people and many nouns
- Broken plurals (جمع التكسير) — Recognize irregular plural patterns
- The dual (المثنّى) — Talk about exactly two of something
- Prepositions (حروف الجر) — Say where, from where, and to where
- Comparative & superlative (أفعل التفضيل) — Compare things
- kāna — 'was / were' (كان) — Put a nominal sentence in the past
- Object pronouns on verbs (رأيتُه، رأيتُها…) — Attach the object directly to the verb
- Numbers 3–10 (العدد والمعدود) — Count things from three to ten

## B1
- The subjunctive (المنصوب) — Say what you want, will, or aim to do
- Jussive & past negation (لم) — Negate the past and give commands with 'let'
- inna and her sisters (إنّ وأخواتها) — Emphasize, report, and give reasons
- The imperative (الأمر) — Give direct commands
- The verbal noun (المصدر) — Turn an action into a noun ('reading', 'to travel')
- Participles (كاتب، مكتوب) — Say who does an action and what it's done to
- Relative clauses (الذي، التي، الذين) — Point out 'the one who / which'

## B2
- The passive voice (المبني للمجهول) — Say an action was done without naming the doer
- Derived verb forms II–IV (فعّل، فاعَل، أفعَل) — Shift a root's meaning: make, do together, cause
- Derived verb forms V–X (تفعّل، انفعل، افتعل، استفعل) — Reflexives, results, and 'seeking' meanings
- The conditional (الشرط: إذا، إن، لو) — Say 'if… then…' — real and unreal
- The circumstantial ḥāl (الحال) — Describe the state something is in during an action
- The specification tamyīz (التمييز) — Clarify 'in what respect' after amounts and comparatives

## C1
- Weak verbs (الأفعال المعتلّة) — Conjugate verbs whose root has و or ي
- The exception (الاستثناء: إلّا، غير، سوى) — Say 'all except…' and 'none but…'
- Vocative & emphasis (النداء والتوكيد بالنون) — Address people formally and swear emphatic promises

## C2
- Fronting & restriction (التقديم، إنّما) — Move a word forward for emphasis; say 'only'
- Literary discourse (أمّا…فـ، قد، لقد) — Structure formal prose: 'as for…', emphasis, and completion
