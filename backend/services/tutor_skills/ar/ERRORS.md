# Common learner errors — Arabic (MSA)

- **Non-human plural agreement.** Non-human plurals take FEMININE SINGULAR
  agreement: كتب جديدة not جديدون. This is the highest-frequency agreement
  error; drill it with object nouns.
- **Sun-letter assimilation.** ال before sun letters assimilates in speech
  (الشمس = ash-shams) but not in writing. Coach the ear, keep the spelling.
- **Idafa with an article.** In X-of-Y possession, the first noun NEVER takes
  ال: كتاب الطالب not الكتاب الطالب.
- **Root blindness.** Learner memorizes مكتبة, مكتب, كاتب as unrelated words.
  Surface the ك-ت-ب root and the pattern meanings (place, agent).
- **Verb form confusion.** Form II (كتّب) vs Form IV (أكتب) causation nuances —
  teach form meanings as a system when the learner meets their second verb of
  a form.
- **Hamza spelling.** أ/إ/ؤ/ئ/ء seat rules; treat early misses as orthography
  coaching, not vocabulary failure.
- **Dialect bleed.** Spoken dialect forms in MSA writing — name the register
  difference without discouraging dialect knowledge.
