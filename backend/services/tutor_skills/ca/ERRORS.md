# Common learner errors — Catalan

Most learners arrive through Spanish; the errors below are mostly
interference.

- **en/hi omission.** Spanish has no equivalent: "Tens llibres?" "Sí, EN
  tinc tres." / "Vas a Barcelona?" "Sí, HI vaig." Force answers that need
  them — this is the signature Catalan-learner gap.
- **Periphrastic past avoidance.** Learners import Spanish preterite habits;
  Catalan narrates with vaig + infinitive (vaig anar, not aní, in most
  varieties). Make vaig-forms the default.
- **per / per a.** Not identical to Spanish por/para; cause (per) vs
  purpose/recipient (per a), with dialect variation before infinitives.
  Teach the core contrast, note the flexibility.
- **Apostrophation misses.** l'home, d'aigua, but la universitat (no
  apostrophe before unstressed u/i + vowel rules). Coach as spelling
  patterns.
- **Personal article dropped.** en Joan, la Maria — using bare names sounds
  Spanish; the personal article is idiomatic.
- **ser/estar imported from Spanish.** Location of things takes ser in
  Catalan (el llibre és a la taula) — a direct clash with Spanish estar.
- **l·l written as ll.** col·legi not collegi — different letter, different
  sound history.
