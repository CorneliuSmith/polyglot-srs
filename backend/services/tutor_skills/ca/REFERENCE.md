# Curriculum reference — Catalan (ca)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Subject pronouns (jo, tu, ell…) — Say who you are talking about — when it needs saying
- Gender and number of nouns — Name people and things, one or many
- Definite articles (el / la / els / les) — Point to a specific thing
- Indefinite articles (un / una) — Mention something for the first time
- Ser — present (soc, ets, és…) — Say who or what someone is
- Tenir — present (tinc gana) — Say what you have — and how you feel
- Present tense of -ar verbs — Talk about what people do
- -re and -ir verbs (perdre, dormir, llegir) — Sleep, read, hear — the other families
- Negation with no — Say what is not so
- Hi ha — there is / there are — Say what exists or is available
- Question words (on, com, què…) — Ask about places, people, time, and reasons
- Adjective agreement — Describe people and things correctly

## A2
- The present perfect (el perfet) — Talk about what has happened (today, recently)
- The past (passat perifràstic: vaig anar) — Talk about completed past actions
- The imperfect (l'imperfet) — Describe ongoing or habitual past actions
- The future (el futur) — Talk about what will happen
- Weak pronouns — direct object (el, la, els, les) — Replace the object with a weak pronoun
- Weak pronouns — en and hi — Replace a quantity/'of it' and a place
- Comparative & superlative (més… que, el més) — Compare things
- Reflexive verbs (rentar-se) — Say the subject acts on itself
- Contractions (al, del, pel) — Combine a preposition with the article
- Possessives & demonstratives (el meu, aquest…) — Say 'my / this' and agree with the noun

## B1
- The subjunctive (el subjuntiu) — Express opinion, wish, doubt, and emotion
- The conditional (el condicional) — Say what you would do; be polite
- Combined weak pronouns (me'l, l'hi, se'n) — Combine two weak pronouns before the verb
- Relative pronouns (que, qui, què, on) — Join two clauses into one
- The imperative (parla! no parlis!) — Give commands and instructions
- The past perfect (el plusquamperfet) — Set one past action before another
- Reported speech (l'estil indirecte) — Report what someone said or asked

## B2
- The passive voice (ser + participi; es passiva) — Say an action is done, without the doer
- Hypotheticals (si + imperfet de subjuntiu) — Say what would happen if…
- The gerund (el gerundi: fent) — Say 'while / by doing' and mark ongoing action
- Subjunctive after conjunctions — Use the subjunctive after 'although, so that, before…'
- Irregular comparatives (millor, pitjor) — Compare with 'better' and 'worse'
- Logical connectors (tanmateix, per tant, ja que) — Link ideas: contrast, cause, consequence

## C1
- Past hypotheticals (condicional passat) — Say what would have happened
- Past subjunctive & sequence of tenses — Match the subjunctive tense to the main verb
- The literary past (el passat simple) — Recognize the simple past of literature

## C2
- Focus & dislocation (és a Roma que…, el llibre l'he llegit) — Foreground a word by moving or doubling it
- Formal register & idiom (registre formal) — Shift between formal/literary and everyday Catalan
