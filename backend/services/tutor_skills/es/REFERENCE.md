# Curriculum reference — Spanish (es)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Subject pronouns (yo, tú, él…) — Say who you are talking about
- Gender and number of nouns — Name people and things, one or many
- Definite articles (el / la) — Point to a specific thing
- Indefinite articles (un / una) — Mention something for the first time
- The verb ser (to be) — present — Say who or what someone is
- The verb estar — location and states — Say where things are and how you feel
- Present tense of -ar verbs — Talk about what people do
- Present tense of -er and -ir verbs — Talk about eating, drinking, living, and more
- Negation with no — Say what is not so
- Hay — there is / there are — Say what exists or is available
- Question words (qué, dónde, cómo…) — Ask about things, places, people, and time
- Adjective agreement — Describe people and things correctly

## A2
- Reflexive verbs (levantarse) — Talk about your daily routine
- Gustar and similar verbs — Say what you like and dislike
- Preterite — regular verbs — Say what you did
- Preterite — key irregulars (fui, hice, tuve) — Tell what happened with the most common verbs
- Imperfect (hablaba) — Describe how things used to be
- Ir a + infinitive (near future) — Talk about plans
- Estar + gerund (present progressive) — Say what is happening right now
- Direct object pronouns (lo, la, los, las) — Avoid repeating what you already mentioned
- Indirect object pronouns (le, les) — Say to whom or for whom
- Comparatives (más… que, tan… como) — Compare people and things

## B1
- Present perfect (he hablado) — Connect the past to now
- Future simple (hablaré) — Make predictions and promises
- Conditional (hablaría) — Say what you would do; soften requests
- Present subjunctive — wishes and requests — Express wants about other people
- Imperative — affirmative and negative (tú) — Give instructions and warnings
- Por vs para — Choose the right 'for'
- Impersonal and passive se — Say what 'one does' or 'is done'
- Double object pronouns (se lo) — Say 'it to him/her/them' smoothly

## B2
- Past (imperfect) subjunctive (hablara) — Talk about unreal or reported wants
- Si-clauses (real and unreal) — Reason about conditions and consequences
- Pluperfect (había hablado) — Order past events
- Reported speech (dijo que…) — Report what people said
- Relative pronouns (quien, cuyo) — Build precise descriptions
- Subjunctive after para que, aunque… — Express purpose and concession

## C1
- Perfect subjunctive (haya hablado) — Judge and doubt completed events
- Pluperfect subjunctive + conditional perfect — Regret and rewrite the past
- Passive with ser + participle — Write in a formal register
- Cleft sentences (fue… quien / lo que) — Put the spotlight exactly where you want it

## C2
- Concessive intensifiers (por más que, digan lo que digan) — Concede forcefully, native-style
- Literary -se subjunctive and ojalá + past — Read and write literary Spanish
- No es que + subjunctive (nuanced denial) — Deny one framing while asserting another
