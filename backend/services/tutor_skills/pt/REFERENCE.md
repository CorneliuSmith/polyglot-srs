# Curriculum reference — Portuguese (pt)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Subject pronouns (eu, você, ele…) — Say who you are talking about
- Gender and number of nouns — Use masculine and feminine, singular and plural
- Articles (o, a, um, uma) — Say 'the' and 'a' with the right gender
- Ser — present (sou, é, somos…) — Say who or what something IS (identity, origin)
- Estar — present (estou, está…) — Say how or where something is right now
- Ter — present (tenho, tem…) — Say what you have — and how old you are
- Present tense of -ar verbs — Talk about habits and current facts
- -er and -ir verbs (comer, abrir) — Use the other two verb families
- Negation with não — Say what is not so
- Tem / Há — there is, there are — Say what exists
- Question words (onde, o que, quando…) — Ask for information
- Adjective agreement — Match adjectives to their noun

## A2
- The past — pretérito perfeito (falei, comeu) — Say what happened (completed actions)
- The imperfect (falava, comia) — Describe how things used to be
- The future with ir (vou falar) — Say what is going to happen
- Contractions (no, da, dele, pelo…) — Merge prepositions with articles and pronouns
- Object pronouns (me, te, o, a, nos) — Replace the object with a short pronoun
- Comparatives (mais… do que, o mais) — Compare things
- Reflexive verbs (me levanto, se chama) — Say the subject acts on itself
- Possessives (meu, sua, dele…) — Say whose something is — without ambiguity
- Demonstratives (este, esse, aquele) — Point at things near me, near you, and far away
- Right now: estar + -ndo (estou falando) — Say what is happening at this moment

## B1
- Presente do subjuntivo (que eu fale) — Express wish, doubt, and emotion
- The imperative (fala! / fale!) — Give instructions and advice
- Tenho falado — the iterative perfect — Say what you've been doing lately
- Relative pronouns (que, quem, onde, cujo) — Join two ideas into one sentence
- The conditional (falaria, gostaria) — Be polite and talk about hypotheticals
- Pronoun placement (me diga / diga-me) — Put the pronoun where Brazilians put it
- Reported speech (disse que, perguntou se) — Report what someone said or asked

## B2
- Futuro do subjuntivo (quando eu falar, se eu quiser) — Talk about future conditions — Portuguese's signature tense
- Imperfeito do subjuntivo (se eu falasse) — Imagine unreal situations
- Infinitivo pessoal (para fazermos) — Conjugate the infinitive — another Portuguese exclusive
- The passive (foi construído; vende-se) — Say something is done without naming the doer
- Ficar — become, stay, be located, keep on — Master Portuguese's most versatile verb
- Connectors (porém, portanto, embora) — Link ideas: contrast, consequence, concession

## C1
- Compound subjunctives (tenha falado, tivesse ido) — Handle completed actions inside subjunctive contexts
- Crase and regência (vou à praia) — Get à right — and the prepositions verbs demand
- Formal pronoun placement (mesóclise and ênclise) — Read and produce the formal written norms

## C2
- Focus & clefts (foi ele que…, é que) — Foreground exactly the word you mean
- Register: Brazil, Portugal, and the written norm — Move between spoken Brazilian, European, and formal writing
