# Curriculum reference — Greek (el)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Subject pronouns (εγώ, εσύ, αυτός…) — Say who you are talking about — when it needs saying
- Gender and number of nouns — Name people and things, one or many
- Definite articles (ο / η / το) — Point to a specific thing
- Indefinite articles (ένας / μία / ένα) — Mention something for the first time
- Είμαι — present (είμαι, είσαι, είναι…) — Say who or what someone is
- Έχω — present (έχω, έχεις, έχει…) — Say what you have — and who's right
- Present tense of -ω verbs — Talk about what people do
- The accusative object (τον / την / το) — Mark what the action lands on
- Negation with δεν — Say what is not so
- Υπάρχει / υπάρχουν — there is, there are — Say what exists or is available
- Question words (πού, τι, πότε…) — Ask about places, people, time, and reasons
- Adjective agreement (καλός / καλή / καλό) — Describe people and things correctly

## A2
- The past — aorist (αόριστος) — Talk about completed past actions
- The genitive case (γενική) — Show possession and relationships
- Plural of nouns (οι φίλοι, οι πόρτες, τα βιβλία) — Make nouns plural
- The imperfect (παρατατικός) — Describe ongoing or habitual past actions
- The future with θα — Talk about what will happen
- Object pronouns (τον, την, το…) — Replace the object with a short pronoun
- Comparative & superlative (πιο…, ο πιο…) — Compare things
- Prepositions (σε, από, με, για…) — Say where, from, with, and for
- Possessives (το βιβλίο μου…) — Say whose something is
- 'want / can / must' + να — Say what you want, can, or must do

## B1
- The subjunctive with να — aspect — Choose one-off vs repeated with να
- The imperative (γράψε! μην γράφεις!) — Give direct commands and prohibitions
- Two object pronouns (μου το έδωσε) — Say 'gave it to me' with both objects
- Relative clauses (που, ο οποίος) — Join sentences with 'who / which / that'
- The mediopassive present (-ομαι) — Say what happens to the subject, or reflexively
- The perfect (παρακείμενος: έχω γράψει) — Say what has happened, with present relevance
- Conditionals with αν — Say 'if… then…' for real possibilities

## B2
- The passive aorist (πλύθηκα, γράφτηκε) — Say a completed action was done to the subject
- Unreal conditionals (αν + past, θα + past) — Talk about what would have happened
- Contracted verbs (αγαπάω / αγαπώ) — Conjugate the -άω/-ώ verb class
- One-word comparatives (καλύτερος, μεγαλύτερος) — Use the synthetic comparative and its irregulars
- Participles (τρέχοντας, γραμμένος) — Add '…-ing' and '…-ed' descriptions
- Reported speech (είπε ότι / να) — Report what someone said or asked

## C1
- Pluperfect & future perfect (είχα γράψει, θα έχω γράψει) — Set one past event before another, or a future completion
- Deponent & irregular verbs (έρχομαι, γίνομαι) — Master common verbs with irregular stems
- Subordinating connectors (επειδή, ενώ, αν και, ώστε) — Build complex sentences with cause, contrast, and result

## C2
- Emphasis & clitic doubling (τον Γιάννη τον ξέρω) — Front a phrase for emphasis and double it with a pronoun
- Register & fixed expressions (formal vs everyday) — Shift between formal and colloquial Greek
