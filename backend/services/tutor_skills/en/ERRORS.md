# Common learner errors — English

Check the learner's native language in their profile and prioritize the
matching sections.

- **Article omission/overuse** (Slavic, Turkish, most African L1s): "I went to
  shop" / "The life is hard." Contrast specific-known (the) vs first-mention
  (a) vs generic (zero). Coach with paired sentences, not rules.
- **Present perfect vs past simple** (nearly everyone): "I have seen him
  yesterday" → saw. Time named → past simple; relevance now → perfect.
- **Third-person -s.** "She go" — small error, high visibility. Quick retry,
  don't lecture.
- **Do-support.** "You like coffee?" is fine in speech; "Like you coffee?"
  (Germanic L1) is not. Drill question formation with do.
- **Preposition selection** (Romance): "depend of", "married with" →
  depend on, married to. Teach verb+preposition as one unit.
- **False friends** (per L1): actual/actualmente ≠ actual/actually (es, pt);
  eventuell ≠ eventually (de). Use their L1 from the profile.
- **Phrasal verb avoidance.** Learners substitute formal Latinate verbs
  (postpone for put off). Fine in writing; coach the informal register too.
