# Curriculum reference — English (en)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Subject pronouns (I, you, he…) — Say who you are talking about
- To be — am / is / are — Say who or what someone is
- Articles: a / an — Mention something for the first time
- The — pointing at a specific thing — Talk about something both of you can identify
- Plural nouns (-s, -es, irregular) — Name more than one of something
- Present simple (I work) — Talk about habits and facts
- Third-person -s (she works) — Talk about what he, she, or it does
- Do-support: questions and negation — Ask and deny in the present simple
- Present continuous (I am working) — Say what is happening right now
- There is / there are — Say what exists or is available
- Question words (what, where, when…) — Ask about things, places, people, and time
- Possessives (my, your, 's) — Say whose something is

## A2
- Past simple — irregular verbs — Say what happened, with the verbs that break the rules
- Present continuous vs present simple — Right now vs in general
- Comparatives and superlatives — Compare two things — or crown the winner
- Going to and will — Two futures: plans vs promises and predictions
- Some, any, much, many — Talk about amounts without counting
- Adverbs of frequency — Say how often — and put the word in the right place
- Can, could, have to — Ability, polite requests, and obligation
- Prepositions of time: in, on, at — Put events on the calendar correctly

## B1
- Present perfect vs past simple — The past that touches now vs the finished past
- First and second conditionals — Real futures and imagined presents
- The passive (is made, was built) — Focus on what happens, not who does it
- Relative clauses (who, which, that, where) — Glue two ideas into one sentence
- Used to — past habits and states — Talk about how things were, but aren't anymore
- Reported speech — Pass on what someone said
- Gerund or infinitive? — Know which verb pattern follows which verb

## B2
- Third and mixed conditionals — Regret and rewrite the past
- Modals of deduction (must, might, can't) — Say how sure you are
- Advanced passives (has been done, being done) — Passives across every tense
- Wish and if only — Regret the present and the past
- Phrasal verbs — separable or not? — Put the object in the right place
- Question tags and indirect questions — Soften questions and check what you believe

## C1
- Inversion (Never have I…, Not only…) — Flip the word order for emphasis
- Cleft sentences (What I need is…, It was… that) — Point the spotlight at one part of the sentence
- Hedging and distancing (would seem, tend to) — Sound measured, academic, and deniable
- Participle clauses (Having finished, …) — Tighten two sentences into one

## C2
- The mandative subjunctive (insist that he be) — The formal demand pattern
- Ellipsis and substitution (so, neither, do so) — Leave out what the listener already knows
- Formal connectors (notwithstanding, albeit) — The registers of written argument
