# Curriculum reference — Turkish (tr)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Personal pronouns and 'to be' (-(y)im, -sin…) — Introduce yourself and others
- Demonstratives (bu, şu, o) — Point things out
- Plural (-ler / -lar) — Talk about more than one
- Question particle (mı / mi / mu / mü) — Turn any statement into a yes/no question
- Locative case (-de / -da) — Say where something is
- Var / yok — there is, there isn't — Say what exists and what doesn't
- Possessive suffixes (-im, -in, -i…) — Say whose something is
- Present continuous (-iyor) — Talk about what is happening now
- Negative present continuous (-miyor) — Say what is not happening
- Accusative case (-ı / -i / -u / -ü) — Mark a specific, definite object

## A2
- Past tense (-di) — Say what happened
- Future tense (-ecek) — Say what will happen
- Dative case (-e / -a) — Say where something is headed
- Ablative case (-den / -dan) — Say where something comes from
- Genitive + possessed (-in … -i) — Link owner and owned
- Aorist / geniş zaman (-ir) — State habits and general truths
- Ability (-ebil) — Say what you can do
- Comparatives (daha / en) — Compare and rank
- Postpositions ile and için — Say with what and for whom
- Before and after (-den önce / sonra) — Order events in time

## B1
- Aorist negative (-mez / -mem) — Deny habits politely and firmly
- Evidential past (-miş) — Report what you didn't witness
- Necessity (-meli) — Say what must be done
- Conditional (-se) — Make if-sentences
- Verbal nouns (-mek / -me) — Use actions as things
- gibi and kadar — Say 'like' and 'as much as'
- While (-ken) — Do two things at once

## B2
- Subject participle (-en) — Describe the one who does
- Object participle (-dik) — Describe the thing that is done
- Passive voice (-il / -in) — Say what is done without the doer
- Causative (-dir / -t) — Have something done
- Reported speech (-diğini söylemek) — Report what people said and know
- Converbs (-ip and -erek) — Chain actions in one sentence

## C1
- The more… (-dikçe) — Say 'the more X, the more Y'
- Without doing (-meden) — Say what happened without something
- Reflexive and reciprocal voice (-in / -iş) — Actions on oneself and each other
- Because (-diği için) — Give reasons formally

## C2
- Formal progressive (-mekte) — Write and speak officially
- Pretending (-mış gibi) — Describe feigned states
- Instead of (-eceğine) — Recommend alternatives
