# Curriculum reference — German (de)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Subject pronouns (ich, du, er…) — Say who you are talking about
- Gender and number of nouns — Name people and things, one or many
- Definite articles (der / die / das) — Point to a specific thing
- Indefinite articles (ein / eine) — Mention something for the first time
- Sein — present (ich bin, du bist…) — Say who or what someone is
- Haben — present (ich habe Hunger) — Say what you have — and how you feel
- Present tense of regular verbs — Talk about what people do
- Verb second (V2) word order — Build German sentences in the German order
- Negation with nicht and kein — Say what is not so
- Es gibt — there is / there are — Say what exists or is available
- Question words (wo, wie, was…) — Ask about places, people, time, and reasons
- Accusative case (den / einen) — Mark the direct object

## A2
- The perfect with haben — Talk about the past in speech
- The perfect with sein — Use sein for movement and change of state
- The dative case (dem, der, den…) — Mark the indirect object — 'to/for whom'
- Modal verbs (können, müssen, wollen…) — Say what you can, must, or want to do
- Simple past of sein & haben (war, hatte) — Say 'was' and 'had' in the past
- Two-way prepositions (in, auf, an…) — Choose accusative for motion, dative for location
- Separable verbs (aufstehen, einkaufen) — Split the prefix off to the end of the clause
- Comparative & superlative (größer als, am größten) — Compare things
- Possessive articles (mein, dein, sein…) — Say 'my / your' and match case & gender
- Subordinate clauses (weil, dass) — Give reasons and report — verb goes to the end

## B1
- The genitive case (des, der) — Show possession — 'of the'
- The simple past (Präteritum) — Narrate the past in writing
- Adjective endings — Give adjectives before a noun the right ending
- Reflexive verbs (sich freuen) — Say the subject acts on itself
- Relative clauses (der, die, das, dem…) — Describe a noun with a whole clause
- The Konjunktiv II (würde, wäre, hätte) — Be polite and talk about hypotheticals
- Infinitive with zu (um … zu) — Link two verbs, and express purpose

## B2
- The passive voice (werden + Partizip II) — Say an action is done, without the doer
- The past perfect (Plusquamperfekt) — Set one past event before another
- Unreal conditions (Konjunktiv II der Vergangenheit) — Say what would have happened
- Connectors & word order (deshalb, trotzdem, damit) — Link ideas and get the verb position right
- Verbs with prepositions & da-/wo- words — Handle fixed verb+preposition pairs and their pronoun forms
- Adjectival nouns (das Gute, ein Reisender) — Turn an adjective or participle into a noun

## C1
- Reported speech (Konjunktiv I) — Report others' words in formal / written German
- Passive alternatives (man, sich lassen, sein + zu) — Avoid the werden-passive with lighter constructions
- Advanced connectors (indem, je … desto, sofern) — Express means, proportion, and condition precisely

## C2
- Extended attributes & nominal style (Nominalstil) — Read and build the dense noun phrases of formal German
- Modal particles (doch, mal, ja, eben) — Colour a sentence the way native speakers do
