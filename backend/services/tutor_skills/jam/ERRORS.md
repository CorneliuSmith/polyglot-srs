# Common learner errors — Jamaican Patois

- **English copula inserted.** "Di uman a nais" — a links NOUNS only;
  adjectives take nothing: Di uman nais. Run the three-way drill: a for
  who-it-is, de for where-it-is, zero for how-it-is.
- **a and de swapped.** "Mi a ya" for "I'm here" — location is de: Mi de
  ya. Equation is a: Mi a di tiicha.
- **Verb endings imported.** "Im rons/ronned/running" — Patois verbs never
  inflect. Tense lives in the markers: im ron / im did ron / im a ron /
  im don ron. Rebuild the sentence with the marker, not the ending.
- **-s plurals.** "di pikni-s" — plural is dem after the noun: di pikni
  dem. With numbers, nothing at all: faiv mango.
- **neva over-read as 'never ever'.** Mi neva si im = "I didn't see him"
  (plain past negative). For emphasis Patois says neva-eva.
- **Double negation 'corrected' away.** Nobadi no si notn is right;
  "Nobadi si eniting" is anglicized. Coach learners to keep the negative
  concord.
- **Possessive 's.** "Jan's buk" — juxtaposition does it: Jan buk. For
  pronouns, fi: fi mi buk, a fi im.
- **se dropped after say/know/hear.** "Im tel mi yu gaan" is understood
  but "Im tel mi SE yu gaan" is the natural clause link.
- **Marker order scrambled.** did a (was doing) is fixed: tense before
  aspect. "a did ron" is wrong; im did a ron.
- **unu avoided.** Learners default to yu for groups; unu is the real
  plural you and locals use it constantly. Push it early.
- **Spelling anxiety.** likkle vs likl, cyaan vs kyaan — both conventions
  are legitimate writing. Grade the WORD, not the spelling system.
