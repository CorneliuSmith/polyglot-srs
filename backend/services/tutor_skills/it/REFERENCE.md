# Curriculum reference — Italian (it)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Subject pronouns (io, tu, lui…) — Say who you are talking about — when it needs saying
- Gender and number of nouns — Name people and things, one or many
- Definite articles (il / la / i / le) — Point to a specific thing
- Indefinite articles (un / una / uno) — Mention something for the first time
- Essere — present (sono, sei, è…) — Say who or what someone is
- Avere — present (ho fame) — Say what you have — and how you feel
- Present tense of -are verbs — Talk about what people do
- -ere and -ire verbs (prendere, dormire, finire) — Take, sleep, finish — the other two families
- Negation with non — Say what is not so
- C'è and ci sono — there is / there are — Say what exists or is available
- Question words (dove, come, che…) — Ask about places, people, time, and reasons
- Adjective agreement — Describe people and things correctly

## A2
- Passato prossimo with avere — Talk about completed past actions
- Passato prossimo with essere — Use essere for movement and reflexive verbs
- The imperfect (l'imperfetto) — Describe ongoing or habitual past actions
- The future (il futuro semplice) — Talk about what will happen
- Direct object pronouns (lo, la, li, le…) — Replace the object with a short pronoun
- Indirect pronouns, ci and ne (gli, le, ci, ne) — Replace 'to someone', a place, or a quantity
- Comparative & superlative (più… di, il più) — Compare things
- Reflexive verbs (lavarsi) — Say the subject acts on itself
- Articulated prepositions (del, al, nel, sul…) — Combine a preposition with the article
- Possessives & demonstratives (il mio, questo…) — Say 'my / this' and agree with the noun

## B1
- The subjunctive (il congiuntivo) — Express opinion, doubt, wish, and emotion
- The conditional (il condizionale) — Say what you would do; be polite
- Combined pronouns (glielo, me lo) — Combine 'it' and 'to someone' before the verb
- Relative pronouns (che, cui, il quale) — Join two clauses into one
- The imperative (parla! non parlare!) — Give commands and instructions
- The past perfect (il trapassato prossimo) — Set one past action before another
- Reported speech (il discorso indiretto) — Report what someone said or asked

## B2
- The passive voice (essere/venire; il si passivante) — Say an action is done, without the doer
- Hypotheticals (se + congiuntivo imperfetto) — Say what would happen if…
- The gerund (il gerundio: facendo) — Say 'while / by doing' and mark ongoing action
- Subjunctive after conjunctions — Use the subjunctive after 'although, so that, before…'
- Irregular comparatives (migliore, meglio, peggio) — Compare with 'better' and 'worse'
- Logical connectors (tuttavia, quindi, poiché) — Link ideas: contrast, cause, consequence

## C1
- Past hypotheticals (condizionale passato) — Say what would have happened
- Past subjunctive & sequence of tenses — Match the subjunctive tense to the main verb
- The literary past (il passato remoto) — Recognize the past of novels and history

## C2
- Focus & dislocation (è a Roma che…, ce l'ho) — Foreground a word by moving or doubling it
- Formal register & idiom (registro formale) — Shift between formal/literary and everyday Italian
