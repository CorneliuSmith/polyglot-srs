# Curriculum reference — Yoruba (yo)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Subject pronouns (mo, o, ó…) — Say who does what
- Tones change meaning — Hear and write the three tones
- The copulas ni and jẹ́ — Say what something is
- Location with wà and ní — Say where people and things are
- Negation with kò — Say what is not so
- Progressive ń — Say what is happening right now
- Plural with àwọn — Talk about more than one
- Questions with ṣé and kí ni — Ask yes/no and 'what' questions
- Possession (mi, rẹ, rẹ̀ / ti) — Say whose something is

## A2
- Perfect ti — Say what has already happened
- Future yóò / máa — Say what will happen
- Object pronouns — Say who receives the action
- Adjectives are verbs (dára, tóbi) — Describe qualities the Yoruba way
- Demonstratives yìí and yẹn — Point out this one and that one
- Imperatives and má (don't) — Tell people to do and not to do

## B1
- Relative clauses with tí — Describe things with 'who/that/which'
- Focus with ni — Emphasize one part of the sentence
- Serial verbs — Chain verbs to express one complex action
- Conditionals with bí … bá — Make 'if' sentences
- Comparatives with ju … lọ — Compare people and things
- Ní vs sí — at a place vs towards it — Say where something is vs where it's heading
- Question words (ta ni, níbo, báwo, èló) — Ask about people, places, manner, price, time, and reasons
- Modals fẹ́, lè, gbọ́dọ̀ (want / can / must) — Add want-to, can, and must to any verb
- Láti + verb (to / in order to) — Chain a purpose or an infinitive onto a verb

## B2
- Habitual máa ń — Talk about routines
- Reported speech with pé — Report what people said and think
- Kí clauses — wishes and indirect commands — Wish, bless, and tell people what should happen
- Numbers after the noun (ìwé méjì, mélòó) — Count things and ask how many
- Making nouns: olù-, oní-/alá-, ì- — Build people-words and idea-words from verbs and nouns
- Serial verbs with fi and fún — Say with-what and for-whom inside the verb chain
- Time clauses: nígbà tí, lẹ́yìn tí, kí … tó — Anchor events before, after, and when

## C1
- Impersonal wọ́n (passive meaning) — Say what 'is done' without a doer
- Although: bí ó tilẹ̀ jẹ́ pé — Concede a point and continue
- Chaining clauses with sì (and then) — String actions into a flowing narrative
- Reduplication: jíjẹ, kíkà, kíákíá — Turn verbs into gerunds and dial up adverbs
- Ideophonic intensifiers (láúláú, roro) — Intensify like a native — each word has its own superlative
- How-clauses with bí … ṣe — Talk about the way something happens

## C2
- Òwe — proverbs, the horses of speech — Cap an argument the way elders do
- Respect register: ẹ and ẹ̀yin — Address elders the way Yoruba demands
- Discourse particles (o, ná, ṣebí, gan-an) — Add the warmth and edge writing can't show
