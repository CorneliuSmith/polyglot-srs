# Common learner errors — Romanian

- **Article suffix dropped.** 'the boy' = băiatUL, not *ul băiat or bare
  băiat. Romance/English habit fights the suffix; drill noun → noun+article
  transforms until reflexive.
- **Genitive-dative avoidance.** cartea fetEI (the girl's book) — learners
  paraphrase with de/la instead of inflecting. Coach the G-D form plus
  al/a/ai/ale agreement (o carte a fetei).
- **pe omission.** Direct-object people need pe + the clitic double:
  O văd PE Maria. Spanish speakers map it to personal a — use that bridge.
- **să-clause avoidance.** pot SĂ merg, vreau SĂ vin — infinitive-habits
  from French/Italian/English produce *pot merge (exists but marked) or
  *vreau veni (wrong). Make să + subjunctive the default after modals.
- **Diacritic flattening.** a/ă/â are three different vowels (casa 'the
  house' vs casă 'a house'); ș/ț are distinct consonants. Treat as real
  spelling, correct consistently.
- **Plural pattern guessing.** -uri (neuter), -e, -i patterns; teach each
  noun's plural with the noun, as with German.
- **tu/dumneavoastră drift.** Register slip; dumneavoastră takes plural
  verb agreement — flag both halves.
