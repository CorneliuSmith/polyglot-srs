# Curriculum reference — Romanian (ro)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Subject pronouns (eu, tu, el…) — Say who you are talking about — when it needs saying
- Gender and number — including the neuter — Name people and things, one or many
- Indefinite articles (un / o) — Mention something for the first time
- The definite article is a suffix (-ul / -a) — Point to a specific thing — the Romanian way
- A fi — present (sunt, ești, este…) — Say who or what someone is
- A avea — present (am, ai, are…) — Say what you have — and how old you are
- Present tense of -a verbs — Talk about what people do
- -ea, -e and -i verbs (a vedea, a merge, a dormi) — See, go, sleep, read — the other families
- Negation with nu — Say what is not so
- Este / sunt — there is, there are — Say what exists or is available
- Question words (unde, ce, când…) — Ask about places, people, time, and reasons
- Adjective agreement — Describe people and things correctly

## A2
- The past — perfectul compus — Talk about completed past actions
- The genitive-dative case — Show possession and the indirect object
- Plural of nouns — Make nouns plural
- The imperfect (imperfectul) — Describe ongoing or habitual past actions
- The future (voi scrie / o să scriu) — Talk about what will happen
- Direct object pronouns (mă, te, îl, o…) — Replace the object with a short pronoun
- Comparative & superlative (mai… decât, cel mai) — Compare things
- Prepositions (în, la, cu, pe, pentru) — Say where, with, for — and mark a personal object
- Possessives (meu, mea, tău…) — Say whose something is
- Reflexive verbs (a se spăla) — Say the subject acts on itself

## B1
- The subjunctive (conjunctivul cu să) — Say what you want, must, or can do
- The imperative (scrie! scrieți! nu scrie!) — Give direct commands and prohibitions
- Dative pronouns (îmi, îți, îi…) — Say 'to me / to you' with a short pronoun
- Relative pronouns (care, ce, cine) — Join sentences with 'who / which / that'
- The conditional (aș scrie) — Say what you would do
- Prepositions with genitive-dative (datorită, în fața) — Use prepositions that govern the genitive or dative
- Reported speech (că, dacă) — Report what someone said or asked

## B2
- The passive voice (este scris / se vinde) — Say an action is done, without naming the doer
- Conditional sentences (dacă…) — Say 'if… then…' — real and unreal
- The gerund (gerunziul: scriind) — Say 'while doing' something
- The supine (de scris, ușor de făcut) — Express 'to be done' and purpose
- Subjunctive after impersonals (trebuie să, e posibil să) — Express necessity, possibility, and emotion
- Comparative structures (tot atât de, din ce în ce) — Express equality and gradual change

## C1
- The pluperfect (mai-mult-ca-perfectul) — Set one past action before another
- The past conditional (aș fi scris) — Say what would have happened
- Subordinating connectors (deși, întrucât, astfel încât) — Build complex sentences: concession, cause, result

## C2
- Clitic doubling (pe Ion îl văd, îi dau Mariei) — Double a full object with a matching pronoun
- Register & the literary layer (perfectul simplu, formal style) — Shift between formal/literary and everyday Romanian
