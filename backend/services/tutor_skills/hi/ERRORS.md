# Common learner errors — Hindi

- **Ergative agreement flipped.** "मैंने किताब पढ़ा" — with ने the verb
  agrees with the object: पढ़ी (किताब is feminine). Rebuild: find the
  object, ask its gender, re-conjugate.
- **ने used with intransitives.** "मैं ने गया" — गया never takes ने.
  Contrast: मैं गया / मैंने खाया. Also the opt-outs: लाना and भूलना refuse
  ने even though they're transitive.
- **Oblique case skipped.** "मेरा घर में" — every noun phrase before a
  postposition goes oblique: मेरे घर में. The possessive shifts too.
- **Copula dropped (Russian/English habit).** "मैं छात्र" — Hindi never
  drops होना: मैं छात्र हूँ. Every present-tense sentence ends in a form
  of होना unless negated.
- **Gender agreement with the speaker forgotten.** A woman saying "मैं
  जाता हूँ" — the participle agrees with the SPEAKER: जाती हूँ. Ask the
  learner to re-say the sentence as themselves.
- **आप with singular verb.** "आप है" — आप always takes plural हैं, even
  for one person. Same for respected elders: पिताजी आए हैं.
- **को-experiencer replaced by English subject.** "मैं पसंद करता हूँ यह"
  — natural Hindi is मुझे यह पसंद है. Rewire like/know/feel verbs to the
  dative frame early.
- **Postposition order anglicized.** "में दिल्ली" — postpositions FOLLOW:
  दिल्ली में. Drill with location triads (में/पर/से).
- **Future -गा agreement ignored.** "वह जाएगा" for a female subject —
  जाएगी. The future carries gender in the गा/गी/गे tail.
- **Nuqta dropped where it changes the word.** ख़ान (Khan) vs खान (mine);
  फन vs फ़न. Point at the dot, model the sound, move on — don't block
  conversation on it.
