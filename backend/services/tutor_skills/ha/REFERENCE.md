# Curriculum reference — Hausa (ha)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Independent pronouns (ni, kai, ke…) — Say who you mean
- Identity with ne / ce — Say what someone or something is
- Completive aspect (na, ka, ya, ta…) — Say what happened (done and finished)
- Continuous aspect (ina, kana, yana…) — Say what is happening
- Future with zan, za ka… — Say what will happen
- Existence: akwai / babu — Say what there is and isn't
- Negation ba … ba — Deny statements
- The genitive linker -n / -r — Link 'X of Y' and possession
- Questions (me, wa, ina) — Ask what, who, and where

## A2
- Common plurals — Name many things
- Object pronouns — Say who receives the action
- Possessive pronouns (-na, -nka…) — Say whose things are
- Imperative and don't (kada) — Give commands and warnings
- Habitual (nakan, yakan…) — Talk about what usually happens

## B1
- Subjunctive (ka tafi, mu je…) — Express wishes, purposes, and suggestions
- Relative clauses (wanda, wadda, waɗanda) — Describe with 'the one who/that'
- Relative continuous -ke — Say 'who is doing' inside descriptions
- Conditionals with idan / in — Make 'if' sentences
- Demonstratives (wannan, wancan, waɗannan) — Point at this one, that one, these and those
- To have: da (ina da, ba ni da) — Say what you have and what you lack
- Describing with mai / masu — Turn any quality into an adjective
- Relative completive (suka, muka, aka) — Use the past inside 'who/that' clauses
- Numbers after the noun (biyu, goma, nawa) — Count things and ask how many
- Choosing the aspect — jiya, yanzu, kowace rana — Pick the right aspect from the sentence's time cues

## B2
- Impersonal an / ana (passive sense) — Say what was/is done without naming who
- Comparison with fi — Say something exceeds another
- Sai — until, then, only, except — Master Hausa's most versatile little word
- Verbal nouns in the continuous (cin, shan, zuwa) — Say what's happening right now, properly
- Because and in order to: don, domin, saboda — Give reasons and purposes
- Before and after: kafin, bayan — Order events in time
- Connectors kuma, har, ma, amma — Link ideas like a storyteller
- Reduplication (biyu-biyu, daban-daban) — Double a word to distribute, vary, or soften it

## C1
- Reported speech with cewa — Report statements and beliefs
- Focus fronting with ne/ce — Emphasize one element
- Ideophonic intensifiers (fari fat, ja wur) — Intensify with the word each quality owns
- Verb pairs: saya/sayar, koya/koyar, dafa/dafu — Flip a verb's direction with its grade
- Indirect objects (mini, maka, masa) — Do things TO and FOR people in one word
- Ɗan-compounds (ɗan kasuwa, ʼyan sanda) — Build person-words with 'son of'

## C2
- Karin magana — proverbs — Season your speech the Hausa way
- Greetings and respect (Ranka ya daɗe) — Greet like a person of manners
- Discourse particles (fa, dai, mana, ashe) — Carry the tone writing can't show
- Ko — or, whether, any-, right? — One little word, a whole toolkit
