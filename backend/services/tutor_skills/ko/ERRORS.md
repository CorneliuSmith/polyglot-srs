# Common learner errors — Korean

- **은/는 where 이/가 belongs (and back).** Answering 누가 왔어요? with
  저는 왔어요 — new information and 'who' answers take 이/가: 제가
  왔어요. Correct by asking what the sentence is ABOUT vs what is NEW.
- **에 for action locations.** "도서관에 공부해요" → 도서관에서
  공부해요. Doing something somewhere is 에서; being or going is 에.
- **Particle stacking.** "저는도 가요" — 도 REPLACES 은/는/이/가, never
  stacks on it: 저도 가요.
- **안 for inability.** "다리를 다쳐서 안 가요" — inability is 못:
  못 가요. 안 says you chose not to.
- **Commands after 아/어서.** "늦어서 빨리 가세요" → 늦었으니까 빨리
  가세요. Only (으)니까 may introduce commands and suggestions.
- **Wrong vowel in the 요-form.** 살다 → "살어요" — bright stems (ㅏ/ㅗ)
  take 아요: 살아요. And ㅂ-irregulars: 춥다 → 추워요, never 춥어요.
- **Full numbers before counters.** "하나 개" → 한 개, "둘 명" → 두 명 —
  하나/둘/셋/넷 shorten before any counter.
- **Sino vs native numbers swapped.** Hours take native (세 시), minutes
  take Sino (삼십 분) — 삼 시 is instantly foreign.
- **반말 to strangers.** Dropping 요 with someone you just met is a
  social error, not a grammar slip — treat it that way, gently.
- **시 on your own actions.** "제가 말씀하셨어요" — honorific 시 raises
  OTHERS; your own acts humble instead: 말씀드렸어요.
- **Pronoun overuse.** Korean drops every recoverable subject; 저는 in
  every sentence sounds like a textbook. Model subject-free replies.
- **English word order leaking.** The verb ends the sentence — always.
  "저는 먹어요 밥을" never survives contact with a native ear.
