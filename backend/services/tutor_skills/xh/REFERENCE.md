# Curriculum reference — Xhosa (xh)

The app's grammar path in teaching order. These point titles are exactly what the learner sees on their cards, and weak grammar items arrive labeled with them — use the same names when coaching, and sequence new material in this order.

## A1
- Subject concords and the present (ndi-, u-, ba-…) — Say who is doing what
- Noun classes 1/2: um-/aba- (people) — Talk about people, one or many
- Noun classes 5/6 and 7/8 — Use the ili-/ama- and isi-/izi- families
- Noun classes 9/10: in-/izin- — Use the in-/izin- family
- Identifying copulatives (ngu-, yi-) — Say 'it is …'
- Present negative (andi-…-i) — Say what is not happening
- Locatives (e-…-ini) — Say where things happen
- Questions (ntoni, bani, phi) — Ask what, who, and where
- To have: -na- — Say what people have

## A2
- Possessive concords (yam, wam, bam…) — Say whose something is
- Recent past (-ile) — Say what (has) happened
- Future (-za ku-) — Say what will happen
- Object concords — Point the verb at its object
- Adjective concords (omhle, entle…) — Describe nouns with agreement
- Demonstratives (lo, le, eli…) — Point out this and that

## B1
- Negative past and future — Deny things in any tense
- Subjunctive (-e) and ma- (let's) — Suggest, urge, and express purpose
- Remote past (a-…-a) — Tell what happened long ago
- Relative clauses (o-…-yo) — Describe with 'who/that/which'
- Noun classes 11, 14, 15 (ulu-, ubu-, uku-) — Handle tongues, abstracts, and actions-as-nouns
- Absolute pronouns (mna, wena, yena…) — Add weight: ME, YOU, HIM
- Persistive -sa- (still / no longer) — Say what's still going on — and what stopped
- Counting with concords (ezimbini, bangaphi) — Count things — and make the number agree

## B2
- Passive (-wa) — Say what is done, without the doer
- Causative (-isa) — Make or help something happen
- Applicative (-ela) — Do something for or to someone
- Instrumental nga- (ngemoto, ngesiXhosa) — Say by, with, in, and on-a-day with one prefix
- When-clauses: xa + participial — Anchor one event on another
- Potential -nga- (ndingakunceda — may I?) — Say can, may, and can't
- Stative -eka / -akala (kufuneka, iyabonakala) — Say what's visible, audible, necessary, available

## C1
- Reciprocal (-ana) — Say people do things to each other
- Conditions with ukuba — Make 'if' and 'that' clauses
- Past continuous (bendifunda, ebetya) — Say what WAS happening
- Purpose: ukuze + subjunctive — Say what something is FOR
- Because: kuba, ngoba — and kutheni (why) — Give reasons and demand them
- Ideophones with ukuthi (wathi cwaka) — Paint sound, silence, and suddenness

## C2
- Amaqhalo — proverbs and ubuntu — Speak with the weight of the elders
- Hlonipha — the respect register — Show respect the isiXhosa way
- Discourse particles (ke, nje, phofu, kaloku) — Carry the tone of a real conversation
- Greetings in full (molo, hamba kakuhle) — Open and close encounters like a local
